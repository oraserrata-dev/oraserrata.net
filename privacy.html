Privacy Policy

Effective: May 19, 2026

Ora Serrata LLC ("we," "us," or "our") builds apps that respect your privacy. This policy describes how our apps and the optional Rouse Relay server software handle your information.

Information We Collect

Our apps are designed to collect as little data as possible, and to keep what they do hold on devices you control. None of the following ever reaches a server operated by Ora Serrata:

  • Device configuration you enter — names, MAC addresses, broadcast IP addresses, and SecureON passwords (if used) for the devices you want to wake
  • Network identifiers for the Wi-Fi networks you save as Locations — SSID and BSSID
  • Relay configuration you enter — relay hostname, port, and auth token, if you set up the optional Rouse Relay

This information is stored locally on your device and, if you have iCloud sync enabled, synced through Apple's iCloud Key-Value Storage to your other Apple devices signed in to the same Apple Account. We have no access to it.

Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, store, transmit, or have any way to access:

  • Your name, email, phone number, or any other personal identifier
  • Your precise geographic location
  • Usage analytics, behavioral data, or telemetry
  • Advertising identifiers
  • The contents of any wake request you send

Permissions Rouse Requests

The app asks for the following operating-system permissions. Each is requested only when the relevant feature first needs it, never at app launch.

  • Local Network (iOS) — required to broadcast Wake-on-LAN magic packets on your Wi-Fi.
  • Location When In Use (iOS and macOS) — Apple gates access to the current Wi-Fi network's name and BSSID behind the Location permission. Rouse uses this only to read which Wi-Fi you're connected to so it can organize devices by Location. Your precise geographic location is never accessed or stored.
  • Notifications — used to confirm wake results and surface routing errors. You can disable this in system Settings at any time.

iCloud Sync

If you are signed in to iCloud and have iCloud sync enabled inside Rouse (the default), your device list and Location list are synced across your Apple devices using Apple's iCloud Key-Value Storage service. This data is stored in your private Apple Account and is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. Ora Serrata does not have access to your iCloud account or its contents.

To stop syncing you can disable iCloud sync inside Rouse's Settings, or remove Rouse's data from iCloud in your device's Settings → Apple ID → iCloud.

Rouse Relay (Self-Hosted)

Rouse Relay is optional server software you can run on your own always-on device — a Mac, a Raspberry Pi, a NAS, a Docker host, or similar — so the app can wake your devices when you're away from home. Ora Serrata does not operate a hosted relay service. Every relay is run by its user on their own hardware, on their own network.

Each wake request sent to your relay contains only:

  • The target device's MAC address
  • The broadcast IP address and UDP port for the local network
  • An optional SecureON password if your device requires one
  • Your relay auth token in the request header, for authentication

The relay does not persist wake requests after they are processed. It writes operational log entries (events like "Wake sent" or "Bad token") to its standard output for your own troubleshooting; these never leave the device you run the relay on.

Diagnostics Shared with Apple

If you have opted in to "Share With App Developers" under Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements on your device, Apple may forward anonymized crash and performance reports to us. We do not run any third-party analytics or crash-reporting SDK. This channel is entirely Apple-mediated and you can turn it off at any time in your device's settings.

Third-Party Services

Rouse contains no third-party analytics, advertising frameworks, tracking SDKs, or social-login integrations. The only network connections the app makes are:

  • UDP broadcasts on your local network, to wake target devices
  • HTTP or HTTPS requests to your own self-hosted Rouse Relay, if you have configured one
  • Apple's iCloud service, mediated entirely by the operating system, for the sync described above

Data Retention and Deletion

Because Rouse stores data only on your device and in your iCloud account, you control retention and deletion completely:

  • To delete data on a single device, uninstall the Rouse app from that device.
  • To delete iCloud-synced data, open Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Account Storage on iOS, or System Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage on macOS, and remove Rouse.
  • The optional Rouse Relay does not retain wake requests; stopping or uninstalling the relay removes its data immediately.

Children's Privacy

Our apps are not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect information from children.

Your Rights

Because we do not collect personal information, most regulatory rights of access, correction, and deletion are satisfied automatically — there is no record of you on our systems to act on. Residents of California, the European Union, the United Kingdom, and other jurisdictions with similar regulations may still contact us at the address below with any privacy-related question.
